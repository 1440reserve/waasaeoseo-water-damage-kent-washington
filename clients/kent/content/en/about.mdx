Kent Water Damage Pros is a local referral service for water damage
emergencies in Kent, Washington. When your basement floods or a pipe bursts
at 2am, you should not have to research restoration companies from your
phone while standing in an inch of water. That is the job we do ahead of
time.

We are not a restoration contractor ourselves. We run this website and a
24/7 emergency line, and we connect you with a licensed, insured local
restoration professional who does the physical work in your home. You always
know which company is coming to your door.

## How the referral service works

When you call the number on this site or submit the form, your request goes
straight to a vetted local crew serving the Kent area. You tell us what
happened and where, we pass those details along, and the crew confirms the
scope and gives you an honest arrival window. Most emergency calls have help
moving within the hour.

The call is answered any time, day or night, because water damage does not
keep business hours. There is no cost to you for the connection and no
obligation to hire anyone. You talk to a real person who can help, not a
recording or a call-center script.

## How we vet the crews

Choosing a contractor in a panic is how people end up with the wrong one. We
do that homework in advance so you do not have to. Before we send anyone
your way, we check that the crew holds a current Washington contractor
registration, carries liability and workers' compensation insurance, and has
a real track record you can look up for yourself. We favor crews that follow
the IICRC water-damage standard, dry with moisture meters instead of
guesswork, and put their scope and pricing in writing.

If a crew stops meeting that bar, we stop referring people to it. Putting one
clear phone number in front of you only helps if the company on the other end
is one we would be comfortable calling for our own home.

## The Kent and South King County area we serve

We cover Kent and the surrounding South King County communities, including
Covington, Des Moines, SeaTac, Tukwila, Fairwood, Maple Valley, and the
neighborhoods across East Hill and West Hill. From the Green River valley
floor, where winter atmospheric rivers push water into basements and crawl
spaces, up to the ridges above it, help is close by. Because the crews are
local, they know the housing stock, the streets that flood, and the fastest
way to your door.

## Documentation for your insurance claim

Most water damage in Kent ends up as an insurance claim, and claims are won
or lost on proof. The crews we connect you with document the loss the way
adjusters expect: photos of the damage before anything is torn out, a written
scope of work, and daily drying logs with moisture readings that show the
house was actually dried, not just left to sit. That record is the difference
between a covered claim and an argument, and it is part of the job, not an
extra you have to ask for.

## Why acting fast matters

Water damage does not wait. Standing water wicks into drywall, subfloor, and
framing by the hour, and mold can begin growing on wet materials within 24 to
48 hours. The single biggest factor in how much of your home you keep is how
fast the water comes out. That is the whole reason we keep the line open
around the clock and vet crews who can actually move when you call.

If you have water damage right now, call. Everything else can wait until the
water is out.
